The University of Hong Kong

Year 1 - Semester 1:

[CAES1000] Core University English

Prof. Vivian Kwan

Grade: A-

Remarks: This course is teaching you how to write acedemic paper. But when Chat-gpt came out, it becomes not worthwhile to spend a lot of time in this course. I think this course help art or social science students better than engineering students.

[CCST9017] Hidden Order in Daily Life: A Mathematical Perspective

Prof. Ng, Tuen Wai; Prof. Zhang, Zhiwen

Grade: A-

Remarks: very interesting common courses. It give your a glimpse of math knowledge behind everyday life. When you took some advanced courses, you will find in amazement that you have been exposed to some of the knowledge in CCST9017. In this course. report and final exam is critical. If you want to get A+, 15/20, 25/30 in report and final respectively is a must.

[ECON1220] Introductory macroeconomics

Prof. Cao Xiyue

Grade: A

Remarks: Easy course but a little hard to get A+. You can start to learn this course two weeks before final exam. No need to do a lot of question. If you understand the essential , final exam is not hard.

[ENGG1310] Electricity and electronics

Prof. Choi, Hoi Wai; Prof. Hou, Yunhe; Prof. Tam, Wai Leuk Vincent

Grade: A

Remarks: The first quiz is a terrible memory. This quiz was the first quiz I had after I come to HKU. I thought it is easy because it is open-book. Without preparation, even having not read through the PPT, I got 5 out of 10, behind the 30 percentile. It really reminds me a lot.

[ENGG1330] Computer programming I

Prof. Chui, Chun kit; Prof. Schnieders, Dirk

Grade: A+

Remarks: I think python’s operation on list and tuple or something like it is very easy to make mistakes, especially it comes to written exam(lol). I have made several mistakes in the final exam. Also, there is a ambiguous problem statement in the final exam “the number of unique vowels”. Luckily, our group project was graded A+ and got the chance to have a presentation in the Grand Hall.

[MATH1853] Linear algebra, probability and statistics

Prof. Han, Guangyue; Prof. Wong, Ngai

Grade: A+

Remarks: The first part of Linear algebra is too fast and the order of knowledge I think is not appropriate. It is better to study MIT18.06 instead. Prof. Strang is wonderful. As for the second part of probability and statistics, it likes the extension of high school knowledge but actually no enough. I still met so many probability related problems when I read papers or learn other advanced computer science courses.
